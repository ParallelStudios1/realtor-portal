# Pitch — Dad's first 5 introductions

This doc is the playbook for going from "I built an app" to "a realtor is paying me $99/month for it." Use it. The exact words matter less than the *order* and the *tone*.

---

## Step 1: The text you send your dad

Send dad this exact text, or close to it, before week 3:

> Hey dad, the realtor portal app I told you about is built and demo-ready. Could you intro me to 3-5 of your realtor clients you think would actually use something like this? I'm not asking you to vouch for the product, just for me — I'll handle the demo and the rest. The pitch is: it's a branded client portal app each firm can rebrand as their own, where their buyers track their deal, see uploaded contracts, get notifications. White-label for individual firms. Works on iPhone and Android. I built the whole thing.
>
> Could you forward the message below to whoever makes sense?

Then attach the email template (next section).

**Why this works:** dad doesn't have to think. He copy-pastes one email per contact. Low friction. He's the one with credibility, you're the one doing the work.

## Step 2: Email template — what dad forwards

Subject: **Quick intro — Turner built something that might be useful for your firm**

> Hi [REALTOR FIRST NAME],
>
> My son Turner has been building a client portal app for real estate firms — basically a private, branded mobile app your buyers can use to track their deal: phase milestones, important dates like closing or appraisal, document drop, messaging with you, etc. Each firm gets it rebranded as their own (logo, name, colors).
>
> He's looking for a few realtors willing to do a 20-minute demo and tell him whether it'd be useful in their actual workflow. No commitment, just feedback. He's 16 but he built the whole thing himself — I'm sharing it with you because I think you'd give him useful, honest input.
>
> Could I connect you two? If yes, just reply and I'll loop him in.
>
> [DAD]

**Why this works:** "20-minute demo, no commitment, just feedback" is a low ask. The age callout is a feature not a bug — most realtors will be curious. Frame it as helping you, not selling them.

## Step 3: Your follow-up email (when dad connects you)

Within 24 hours of dad's intro, you send:

> Hi [REALTOR FIRST NAME] — Turner here, thanks for being open to a chat.
>
> Quick context: it's a branded mobile app (iOS + Android) where your clients see their deal phase, important dates, and any documents you upload, plus they can message you directly. Each firm I work with gets it rebranded — your logo, name, colors. Apple Store / Play Store.
>
> Easiest is a 15-min Zoom — I'll share my screen and walk through the realtor side and the client side. Are any of these times open?
> - [TIME 1, in their timezone]
> - [TIME 2]
> - [TIME 3]
>
> If those don't work, name a time and I'll be there. Or if it's faster, here's a 90-second video showing the app in action: [LINK].
>
> Either way, no commitment from you — I just want feedback from someone who'd actually use this.
>
> — Turner

**Why this works:** specific times kill the "I'll get back to you" loop. The video link is a fallback for the busy ones who'll never schedule a call but might watch a 90-sec demo and reply.

## Step 4: The demo call (15-20 min)

### First 60 seconds — establish credibility

> "Thanks for the time. Quick context on me — I'm 16, I run [Parallel Studios], I've been building software for [X years]. I built this entire app — front end, back end, design. My dad mentioned you'd be a good person to give me real feedback."

Don't apologize for being young. State it, move on.

### Next 5 minutes — demo

Walk through this exact order. Share screen.

1. **Realtor side first.** "This is what you'd see when you log in." Show clients tab → tap one client → show phase stepper, the import dates list, the document list, the activity feed.
2. "Watch what happens when I move this client from 'Searching' to 'Offer Made.'" Tap the phase. The activity row "[You] moved Deal Phase" appears.
3. Switch to the client view (you have a second test account on a second phone or simulator). "This is what your client sees on their phone." Show the same phase update they just got pushed.
4. Show the milestone celebration if you got time to wire it up: "When their phase changes to under contract, they see this."
5. Upload a PDF on the realtor side. Switch back to client. The PDF is there.

### Next 10 minutes — listen, do not pitch

This is the most important part. Ask:

- "Walk me through how you currently keep clients informed during a deal." (Listen for: text messages, emails, no system, dropped balls.)
- "What's the biggest thing your clients ask you that you wish you didn't have to answer 50 times?" (Likely: "What's happening with my offer?", "When's closing again?", "Did the inspection happen yet?")
- "Of what I just showed, what would you actually use? What's missing? What's there but you'd never use?"
- "If I gave you this for your firm and your clients next week, branded with your logo and name, what would you want me to charge?"

That last question is gold. Their number is a *signal*, not a target. If everyone says $50/month, you have less pricing power. If they all say $200+, you're underpricing.

### Last 2 minutes — close the loop

> "Really helpful, thank you. Two things — first, if you wouldn't mind, who else at your firm or another firm should I show this to? And second, would you want me to come back when [TOP FEATURE THEY ASKED FOR] is live?"

That second question is your hook back. They almost always say yes.

## Step 5: After the call

Within 1 hour:
- Write down 5 bullets: what they liked, what they hated, what they'd pay, who they referred, follow-up date.
- Send a 3-line thank-you email. "Thanks again. Here's the demo video [LINK] in case you want to share with anyone. Following up on [DATE] when [FEATURE] is live."

## Pricing position when asked

If they ask outright "how much will it cost":

> "For the first few firms it's $99/month flat for everything. We'll review pricing once the product is more mature. If you want to be one of the first 3, that price is locked for as long as you stay."

Reasons:
- $99 is psychologically a single-decision price.
- "Locked for life" is sticky.
- Bigger firms might balk at *any* number — you can negotiate up to $199 if pushed.
- Don't go below $50. You'll attract the wrong customers.

## What to NEVER do on these calls

- Promise a feature in a timeline you can't hit. "On the roadmap" is fine. "We'll have it next month" is a lie waiting to happen.
- Negotiate against yourself. If they say "could you do $50?" don't say "I could go to $80." Say "the $99 is what makes this work for me — but I can lock that price if you commit to a year."
- Bash competitors by name. Some realtors love kvCORE / Follow Up Boss / Real Geeks. Don't insult their tools.
- Talk about the technology stack. They don't care.
- Promise to build them a custom version. White-label means same app, different brand. Custom features per firm = death.
